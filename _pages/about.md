 My name is Arthur Powanwe. I am a postdoctoral associate in the Maths Department at Western University (Canada), working in the supervision of Prof. Lyle Muller. Previously, I was a postdoctoral associate at the University of Ottawa. Before that, I did my PhD in Physics and Computational Neuroscience at the University of Ottawa under the supervision of Prof. Andre Longtin.

My research lies at the intersection of Aplied mathematics, computational neuroscience, and Machine learning. During my Ph.D, I used stochastic analysis to investigate the dynamics and statistics of fast brain rhythms known as gamma rhythms and their capacity to share sensory information. In my current position at Western University, I investigate the computational properties of neural rhythms, amongst other theoretical questions in nonlinear dynamics, graph theory, and machine learning.
